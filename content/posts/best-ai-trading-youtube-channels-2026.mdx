YouTube has become the default classroom for retail traders who want to learn AI trading. The problem is that the algorithm often rewards drama over education. Channels that shout about secret algorithms get more views than channels that debug pandas errors. If you want to actually learn, you need a curated list.

This guide ranks the best AI trading YouTube channels to follow in 2026. The ranking is based on educational value, transparency, code quality, and realistic expectations. No channel below is perfect, but each one teaches something useful without promising you a Lamborghini by Friday.

## Ranking Criteria

Before the list, here is how we judged each channel. We believe these criteria matter more than subscriber count:

| Criterion | Why It Matters | What We Looked For |
|-----------|----------------|--------------------|
| Educational clarity | Ideas should be explained step by step | Structured videos, clear examples |
| Code transparency | Real learning requires seeing how things work | Shared notebooks, GitHub links, or code walkthroughs |
| Realistic expectations | Trading is hard and uncertain | Honest discussion of losses, drawdowns, and risks |
| Consistency | One good video is not enough | Regular uploads with coherent themes |
| Conflict disclosure | Trust requires honesty | Clear statements about sponsorships or affiliations |

Channels were scored across these dimensions and sorted by overall usefulness for retail traders. If you are new to this space, start with our [AI trading for beginners](./ai-trading-for-beginners) guide before diving into channels.

## Channel Deep Dives

### Part Time Larry

Larry's channel is one of the best entry points for Python-based trading. His videos move slowly, explain every line of code, and assume almost no prior knowledge. If you have never installed pandas, this is where you start. His weakness is that some examples are toy strategies, so treat them as learning material, not production systems.

### Robot Wealth

Robot Wealth focuses on the process of becoming a quantitative trader rather than handing out hot tips. The hosts discuss data hygiene, strategy development workflows, and the psychology of systematic trading. The content is less code-heavy and more conceptual, which makes it ideal once you have basic programming skills.

### Algo Trading With Kevin

Kevin's channel stands out for its honesty about what bots can and cannot do. He reviews popular tools, explains why many fail, and shares realistic performance metrics. This is the channel to watch when you are tempted to buy a "done for you" AI trading system.

### Asirikuy

Asirikuy is for traders who want to run live systems. The channel covers execution infrastructure, risk management, and the operational side of automated trading. The content is advanced, but it is also grounded in real experience rather than theory.

### Quantitative Research and Trading

This channel leans academic. Expect discussions of statistical arbitrage, machine learning validation, and portfolio theory. It is not beginner-friendly, but it is excellent for intermediate traders who want to understand why strategies work or fail.

### VibeTrading

Our own channel focuses on open-source tools, Alpaca paper trading, and critical thinking about AI trading claims. We publish tutorials that match the written guides on this site, so you can follow along with real code.

## Channels to Skip

Just as important as knowing what to watch is knowing what to avoid. Here are red flags that should make you unsubscribe immediately:

- Guaranteed daily or weekly returns
- Constant display of luxury cars, watches, or mansions
- Refusal to show code, data, or verified results
- Affiliate links without disclosure
- Aggressive sales funnels to expensive courses

## How to Build a Watch Routine

Subscribing to channels is easy. Learning from them is harder. Here is a simple routine:

1. Pick one channel that matches your current level.
2. Watch one video and pause to take notes.
3. Reproduce any code in your own environment.
4. Change one variable and observe what happens.
5. Summarize the lesson in one sentence before moving on.

If you are not writing code or taking notes, you are probably consuming content rather than learning. Be honest with yourself about which one you are doing.

The best AI trading YouTube channels in 2026 are the ones that treat trading as a craft, not a performance. They show code, discuss losses, and avoid easy promises. Use this list as a starting point, but always verify ideas by backtesting and paper trading them yourself.
